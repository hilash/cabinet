# Moonbeam Meltdrops

**"The candy that turns an ordinary evening into something you'll owl your friends about."**

This is our hero product for Q2 — a shimmery silver candy that dissolves on your tongue and makes it glow with a soft, ethereal silver light for approximately 45 seconds. It's beautiful. It's romantic. It's the first Weasley product that's genuinely *elegant*, and Fred is still slightly uncomfortable about that.

---

## Product Details

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Flavor** | Elderflower and stardust (yes, actual stardust — sustainably harvested) |
| **Visual Effect** | Tongue glows silver; faint shimmer around lips |
| **Duration** | 45 seconds |
| **Ministry Rating** | Safe (Class A — Decorative Enchantment) |
| **Price** | 7 Galleons (Premium tier) |
| **Target Market** | Date night, Valentine's gifts, "impress someone" occasions |
| **Shelf Life** | 6 months (shimmer fades after 9 months but flavor holds) |

## Why This Product Matters

Moonbeam Meltdrops represent a strategic expansion beyond pure pranks. The date-night market is enormous, underserved, and willing to pay premium prices. George identified the opportunity after noticing that every Valentine's Day, customers asked "do you have anything... nice?"

We do now.

## Development Story

George spent four months perfecting the elderflower-stardust balance. Too much stardust and the glow is blinding (romantic mood: ruined). Too little and it's just a fancy candy (brand promise: broken). The current formula hits the sweet spot — literally.

Fred's contribution was the tagline and the idea to make the packaging deep midnight blue with silver foil. Even George admits it's "annoyingly good."

## Marketing Angle

- **Primary:** "Light up your evening" — romantic, premium, gift-worthy
- **Secondary:** "The most beautiful thing you'll ever eat" — sensory, experiential
- **Tertiary:** "Weasley quality, but make it elegant" — brand evolution story

## Known Issues

- Glow can be startling if the consumer doesn't expect it. Label clearly states "YOUR TONGUE WILL GLOW. THIS IS INTENTIONAL."
- Slight interaction with Butterbeer causes extended glow (up to 2 minutes). Feature, not bug.
- Fred keeps eating the test batches. This is a production issue.
